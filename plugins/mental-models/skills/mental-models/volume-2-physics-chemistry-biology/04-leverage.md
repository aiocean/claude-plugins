# Leverage

> "Give me a lever long enough and a fulcrum on which to place it, and I shall move the world."
> — Archimedes

## Core Concept

**Leverage** is the ability to amplify force—getting outsized results from small inputs. A lever multiplies the effort you put in, allowing you to accomplish what would otherwise be impossible.

As a mental model, leverage is about finding points where small actions produce disproportionately large results.

## The Fundamental Truth

Not all effort is equal. Some actions produce 10x or 100x the results of others. The key to extraordinary outcomes is finding and applying leverage.

**Without leverage:** Linear relationship between effort and results
**With leverage:** Exponential relationship between effort and results

## Types of Leverage

### 1. Physical Leverage
The original form. Tools multiply physical force:
- Crowbar pries open what hands cannot
- Wheel reduces effort to move heavy loads
- Pulley system lifts what muscles cannot

### 2. Financial Leverage
Using borrowed capital to amplify returns:
- $100K down payment controls $500K property
- Business loans fund growth beyond current capital
- Margin trading (high risk, high reward)

**Warning:** Financial leverage amplifies losses too.

### 3. Time Leverage
Getting more done per unit of time:
- Delegation (others' time for your output)
- Automation (machines' time for your output)
- Systems (processes that run without you)

### 4. Knowledge Leverage
Information asymmetry creates advantage:
- Expertise commands higher prices
- Knowing what others don't enables better decisions
- Education compounds over a lifetime

### 5. Technology Leverage
Digital tools multiply reach and capability:
- Software can serve millions at marginal cost near zero
- One piece of content reaches unlimited audience
- Automation handles repetitive tasks at scale

### 6. Relationship Leverage
Networks multiply opportunity:
- Introductions open doors
- Reputation precedes you
- Trust enables deals that contracts cannot

## The New Forms of Leverage (Naval Ravikant)

**Old Leverage (Permissioned):**
- Capital (need money or investors)
- Labor (need to hire and manage people)

**New Leverage (Permissionless):**
- Code (write it yourself, deploy infinitely)
- Media (create it yourself, distribute globally)

**Key insight:** Code and media are leverage you can build while you sleep.

## Finding Your Leverage Points

### High-Leverage Activities

| Domain | High Leverage | Low Leverage |
|--------|--------------|--------------|
| **Business** | Systems that scale | One-off custom work |
| **Career** | Skills in high demand | Easily replaceable tasks |
| **Marketing** | Content that spreads | Paid ads that stop when you stop |
| **Learning** | Foundational principles | Trivia and details |
| **Relationships** | Deep connections | Shallow networks |

### The Leverage Test

For any activity, ask:
1. If I do this well, what's the potential upside? (10x? 100x?)
2. Does this compound over time?
3. Does success here multiply success elsewhere?
4. Can this be done once and pay off repeatedly?

## Applying Leverage in Business

### Product Leverage
- Build once, sell infinitely (software, digital products)
- Create assets that generate ongoing revenue
- Invest in systems, not just labor

### Marketing Leverage
- Content that continues working (SEO, evergreen articles)
- Word of mouth (customers do the selling)
- Platforms that amplify (distribution channels)

### Operational Leverage
- Automation of repetitive tasks
- Templates and checklists
- Training that multiplies capability

### Strategic Leverage
- Owning a bottleneck (what does everyone need?)
- Network effects (value increases with users)
- Switching costs (hard to leave once adopted)

## Applying Leverage in Personal Life

### Career Leverage
- Build rare, valuable skills
- Create once, benefit forever (writing, teaching)
- Choose roles where results scale (not hours worked)

### Learning Leverage
- Master fundamentals that apply everywhere
- Learn to learn faster
- Build mental models (knowledge that compounds)

### Relationship Leverage
- Know people who know people
- Maintain relationships that are mutually beneficial
- Build reputation that opens doors

### Health Leverage
- Good sleep multiplies everything else
- Exercise compounds mental and physical capacity
- Good habits prevent expensive problems

## The Leverage Scorecard

Rate activities on a 1-10 scale for:

**Impact:** How much does success here matter?
**Scalability:** Does this multiply, or is it one-time?
**Durability:** Does the benefit last?

Focus on activities scoring highest across all three.

## Dangers of Leverage

### 1. Leverage Amplifies Everything
- Good decisions → Great outcomes
- Bad decisions → Catastrophic outcomes
- Financial leverage can bankrupt you
- Reputation leverage can make scandals devastating

### 2. Leverage Can Be Taken Away
- Platforms can change rules
- Employees can leave
- Capital can be recalled
- Relationships can sour

### 3. Leverage Requires Maintenance
- Systems need updating
- Skills need refreshing
- Relationships need nurturing
- Capital needs protecting

## Building Your Leverage Stack

### Step 1: Identify Your Current Leverage
What assets, skills, or relationships currently multiply your efforts?

### Step 2: Identify Leverage Gaps
Where are you missing leverage that would help?

### Step 3: Build Systematically
- Short-term: Use available leverage better
- Medium-term: Develop new skills and relationships
- Long-term: Build systems and assets that compound

### Step 4: Protect Your Leverage
Don't neglect what's already working.

## Key Takeaways

1. **Seek disproportionate returns** - Find where small inputs create large outputs
2. **New leverage is permissionless** - Code and media don't require permission
3. **Leverage compounds** - The right leverage today enables more leverage tomorrow
4. **Leverage amplifies both directions** - Be careful with what you're multiplying
5. **Build leverage systematically** - It's a skill that develops over time

## The Ultimate Leverage Question

Ask regularly:

**"What could I do today that would still be paying off in 10 years?"**

That's where leverage lives.

## Related Models

- **Compounding** - Leverage + time = exponential growth
- **Bottlenecks** - Finding leverage points in systems
- **80/20 Principle** - Most results come from leveraged few
